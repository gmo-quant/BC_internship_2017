CCN: ASL2160 Title: American Sign Language Iii Credit Hrs: 4.00
Description : 
Students will acquire advanced linguistic principles of American Sign Language and vocabulary, both expressively and receptively. Classes are conducted in ASL at an advanced level and pace without voice-interpretation. Cultural literacy will be enhanced related to Deaf Studies and Deaf Culture through interactions within the Deaf community. Linguistic activities & exercises supplemented by laboratory and/or multi-media presentations will be utilized to develop communicative fluency and an appreciation for cultural diversity. (This course is designed for students who have excelled in ASL I and ASL II as content builds upon the foundation laid in those prerequisite courses. Students should check individual university program requirements for transferability.)
 
Prerequisite(s) : ASL1150
